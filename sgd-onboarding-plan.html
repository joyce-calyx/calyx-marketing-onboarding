Onboarding Plan

Senior Graphic Designer

Calyx Containers Reports to Alex Joyce Days 1–90 Confidential
Overview

The Senior Graphic Designer joins Calyx Containers at a moment when visual execution has never mattered more. The brand is in the early stages of a rebrand, tradeshow creative demands are high, and the sales team needs a complete enablement kit that doesn't yet exist. The design function has room to grow, and you are stepping into this role to make an immediate, visible impact.

This plan is built so the Senior Graphic Designer becomes the creative engine of the marketing team before reaching for brand strategy input. The path moves through three deliberate phases: orient and absorb in the first 30 days, contribute real creative work in days 31–60, and take full ownership of design production by day 90.

The role requires close collaboration from day one with Alex Joyce on brief and direction, with touch points across all teams including learning from the sales team directly regarding what enablement assets they actually need to be successful in the field. None of this work is done in isolation.

High level outcomes

By day 90, the Senior Graphic Designer should be a fully embedded creative contributor — not still orienting. The outcomes below define what success looks like at the close of this plan.

Independently owns sales enablement design, tradeshow creative, social templates, ABM visuals, and the design asset library
Brand system fluent — applying Calyx visual identity accurately across all asset types without routine sign-off
Led creative production for at least one tradeshow end-to-end, from brief to on-site execution
Built and shared a design AI workflow reference used as a team resource
Contributed visible creative work to at least four of five live Q2 marketing OKRs without owning delivery of any
Baseline Sales Enablement Kit scoped, planned, and Phase 1 in flight as the role's Initial Project
Collaborated with Senior Marketing Manager to build updated marketing assets structure in Google Drive
Three concrete design process improvement proposals submitted; design asset library v1 organized and documented

The 30 / 60 / 90 plan

Phases

"The most observant designer in every room."

Access & admin
Complete all HR onboarding. Confirm and test access to the full design and marketing stack — Adobe CC, Figma, Google Drive, Gmail, Slack, HubSpot, Gong, Shopify, Claude/ChatGPT/Gemini. Flag any gaps to Mirhaan Rajani by end of Week 1.
Brand immersion
Deep-dive into Calyx brand guidelines — visual identity, typography, color system, tone of voice. Review all active asset types: sales decks, tradeshow materials, social templates, and print collateral. Understand the design system top to bottom before producing anything.
Relationships
1:1s with Alex Joyce, Peter Andersson (VP Product), Simon Knobel (CEO), and Alex Gonzalez. Introductions to all four agency partners: Beard Bros, Grasslands, Creative Bend, CRC. All cross-functional intros complete by Day 30.
Production process
Half-day with Amie Hadley on the end-to-end production ticket workflow — brief submission, prepress review, print specs, fulfillment, and shipping. Plant walk with Greg Furner to understand capacity and collateral lead times. AI training session with Xander on platforms, use cases, and design-specific tools.
30-day checkpoint
  • All agency partner relationships established with warm intros from Alex Joyce
  • Full design and marketing stack access tested and functional
  • Brand guidelines reviewed; first asset produced and approved
  • Production workflow understood — ticket to fulfillment — with Amie Hadley sign-off
  • Written agreement with Alex Joyce on what creative transfers on Day 31

"Real creative work begins. Quality before speed."

Sales enablement
Co-produce sales assets with Alex Joyce. Begin contributing to the master deck, product one-sheeters, and account-specific materials. Strategy and messaging direction stay with Alex Joyce through Day 60.
Tradeshow creative
Co-lead creative production for the next show — booth graphics, signage, printed collateral, and digital assets. Coordinate with Amie Hadley on print specs and lead times. Write a post-show retro on what worked and what didn't.
Social templates
Build or refine the visual template library for social channels. Ensure all templates are brand-accurate, editable, and documented in the design asset library.
ABM visuals
Produce account-specific visual assets for target accounts in the HubSpot ABM board. Coordinate with the Marketing Coordinator on which accounts are active and what assets are needed.
Design asset library
Begin the audit and reorganization of the Google Drive design folders. Document naming conventions, folder structure, and version standards. Announce changes before restructuring shared folders.
AI integration
Explore design-specific AI tools (Firefly, Midjourney, ChatGPT). Document three concrete AI workflows. Build the design AI one-sheeter v1 for team reference.
60-day checkpoint
  • Co-leading sales enablement, tradeshow creative, social templates, ABM visuals, and design asset library
  • One tradeshow's creative production complete with a written post-show retro
  • Brand system fluent — producing routine assets without sign-off from Alex Joyce
  • Three AI design workflows documented; design AI one-sheeter v1 published
  • Visible creative contributor on at least three live Q2 OKRs

"The creative engine. Owns design production without prompting."

Full design ownership
Own sales enablement design, tradeshow creative, social templates, ABM visuals, and the design asset library — all end-to-end with documented brief-to-delivery processes.
Tradeshow creative lead
Run the next show's creative independently. Alex Joyce attends in support. Full creative motion documented: brief, design, print coordination, on-site, post-show.
Initial Project: Sales Enablement Kit
Scope by Day 75. Build the project plan with deliverables, contributors, and target completion. Kick off Phase 1 — this demonstrates how the role scopes, plans, and ships real design work with cross-functional input.
90-day memo
Written memo to Alex Joyce: brand gaps observed, design process improvements, top three recommendations, Q3 creative priorities, and the Sales Enablement Kit plan. The bridge from onboarding into running the design function.
90-day checkpoint
  • Independently owns all five core design workflows with documented processes
  • Led one tradeshow creative end-to-end with a documented show motion
  • Visible creative contributor on at least four of five active Q2 OKRs
  • Baseline Sales Enablement Kit scoped, planned, and Phase 1 in flight
  • 90-day memo delivered; three design process improvement proposals submitted
  • Design asset library v1 organized, documented, and announced to the team

Risks to avoid

For the designer and manager
Designer
Don't redesign the brand in the first 30 days
Absorb the existing system first. Document gaps and propose improvements in the 90-day memo — not in week two.
Designer
Don't produce without a brief through Day 60
Every asset needs a brief from Alex Joyce until the design-to-delivery process is established and trusted.
Designer
Don't take on every design ask
Use Alex Joyce as a scope filter through Day 60. After that, "let me check capacity" before "yes."
Designer
Don't try to own a Q2 OKR
This role is a creative contributor across multiple OKRs — not the delivery owner of any in Q2.
Manager
Don't transfer Q2 OKR delivery
Those OKRs belong to Alex Joyce. Pressure to "just have them run with it" derails the creative ramp.
Manager
Don't skip written creative briefs
Verbal briefs produce misaligned work. Every asset needs a brief — even for quick turnaround requests.
Manager
Don't maintain back-channels with agencies
Route all agency creative requests through the Senior Graphic Designer starting Day 31 — even when it's tempting not to.
Manager
Don't skip brand orientation time
Rushing to production before brand immersion is complete produces assets that require rework. Protect the first 30 days.
