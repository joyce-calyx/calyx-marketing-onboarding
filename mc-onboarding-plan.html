Onboarding Plan

Marketing Coordinator

Calyx Reports to Alex Joyce Days 1–90 Internal Use
Overview

The Marketing Coordinator joins Calyx at a moment of real momentum. The marketing team is supporting a multi-front Q2. We're working on a 12-week dram pricing campaign, the Cure $340k push, and a Calyx rebrand, alongside sustained tradeshow rhythm, ABM execution, and realigning on social media planning and execution.

The role is fundamentally about translating marketing plans into reliable, on-brand execution. This plan is built so the Marketing Coordinator becomes operationally indispensable before reaching for strategic input. The plan moves through three deliberate phases: connect and listen in the first 30 days, commit to real work streams in days 31–60, and taking full ownership by day 90.

This plan is here to set this role up for success: build workflows and product education, transition responsibilities deliberately, and avoid filling the calendar with every adjacent ask the marketing team receives.

High level outcomes

By day 90, the Marketing Coordinator should be a fully embedded operational contributor — not still ramping. The outcomes below define what success looks like at the close of this plan.

Independently owns sample kits, social cadence, blog/SEO, ABM support, Drive organization, and tradeshow logistics
Demo-fluent across all four product lines: Flexible Packaging, Rigid Packaging, Calyx Cure, and Labels
Supported two tradeshows — one as contributor, one with full logistics ownership end-to-end
Built and shared a Marketing AI Platforms one-sheeter (Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini) used as a team reference
Contributed visible work to all five live Q2 marketing OKRs without owning delivery of any
Production Field Guide scoped, planned, and Phase 1 in flight as the role's Initial Project
Delivered a written 90-day observation memo covering team strengths, process gaps, and recommended improvements
Three concrete process improvement proposals submitted; marketing operations handbook v1 shared with the team

Risks to avoid

For the coordinator and manager
Coordinator
Don't redesign in the first 30 days
Listen first; document later; propose improvements in writing in the 90-day memo.
Coordinator
Don't speak for the brand without sign-off
Voice and positioning belong to Alex Joyce until formally handed off.
Coordinator
Don't take on every "small" ask
Use Alex Joyce as a filter through Day 60. After that, "let me check capacity" before "yes."
Coordinator
Don't try to own a Q2 OKR
This role is a contributor across multiple OKRs — not the delivery owner of any in Q2.
Manager
Don't transfer Q2 OKR delivery
Those OKRs belong to Alex Joyce. Pressure to "just have them run with it" derails the ramp.
Manager
Don't skip written hand-offs
Each workstream transition needs a one-pager. Verbal hand-offs get re-litigated.
Manager
Don't maintain back-channels
Route everything through the Marketing Coordinator starting Day 31 — even when it's tempting not to.
Manager
Don't skip warm partner intros
Cold partner introductions take weeks to warm. A warm hand-off from Alex takes a day.
